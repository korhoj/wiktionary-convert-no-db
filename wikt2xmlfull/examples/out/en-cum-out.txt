uncountable	\nEtymology\n
{{prefix|en|un|countable}}

\n\n\na.\n1 So many as to be incapable of being counted.\n2 (lb en mathematics) Incapable of being put into one-to-one correspondence with the natural numbers or any subset thereof.\n3 (lb en grammar of a noun) That cannot be used freely with numbers or the indefinite article, and therefore :Appendix:Glossary#uncountable takes no plural form. Example: ''information''.\nn.\n(lb en grammar) An uncountable noun.
