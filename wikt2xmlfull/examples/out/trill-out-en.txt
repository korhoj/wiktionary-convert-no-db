trill	\nEtymology\n
From {{etyl|enm|en}} {{term|trillen|lang=enm}}. Compare Norwegian {{term|trille|lang=no}}, Swedish {{term|trilla|lang=sv}}.

\n\n\nn.\n1 (context music English) A rapid alternation between an indicated note and the one above it, in musical notation usually indicated with the letters ''tr'' written above the staff.\n2 (context phonetics English) A type of consonantal sound that is produced by vibrations of the tongue against the place of articulation, for example, Spanish ''rr''.\nvb.\n1 (context intransitive English) To create a trill sound; to utter trills or a trill; to play or sing in tremulous vibrations of sound; to have a trembling sound; to quaver.\n2 (context transitive English) To impart the quality of a trill to; to utter as, or with, a trill.\n3 (context intransitive obsolete English) To trickle.
