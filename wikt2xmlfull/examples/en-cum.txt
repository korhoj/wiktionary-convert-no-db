====Usage notes====
Many style guides and editors recommend the spelling ''[[come]]'' for verb uses (''to orgasm''/''to ejaculate'') while strictly allowing the spelling ''cum'' for the noun (''semen''/''female ejaculatory discharge''). Both spellings are sometimes found in either the noun or verb sense, however. Others prefer to distinguish in formality, using ''come'' for any formal usage and ''cum'' only in slang, erotic or pornographic contexts.&lt;ref&gt;[http://www.glossophilia.org/?p=4925 Glossophilia]&lt;/ref&gt;

The past tense and past participle variant ''cummed'' is used when the verb is felt as a denominal from the noun rather than a specialized sense of the verb ''come''.

# So [[many]] as to be incapable of being [[count]]ed.
#: ''The reasons for our failure were as '''uncountable''' as the grains of sand on a beach.''

#: ''Cantor’s “diagonal proof” shows that the set of real numbers is '''uncountable'''.''
# {{lb|en|grammar|of a noun}} That cannot be used freely with numbers or the [[indefinite article]], and therefore [[:Appendix:Glossary#uncountable|usually]] takes no [[plural]] form. Example: ''[[information]]''.

#: ''One meaning in law of the usually '''uncountable''' noun &quot;information&quot; is used in the plural and is countable.''
