===Etymology===
{{root|en|ine-pro|*peh₂w-}}
{{etymon|en|id=reference work|bor|NL.&gt;encyclopaedīa&gt;encyclopedia|tree=1}}
{{bor+|en|NL.|encyclopēdīa, [[encyclopaedia|encyclopaedīa]]|t=general education}}, a [[univerbation|univerbated]] form of {{der|en|grc-koi|ἐγκύκλιος παιδείᾱ|t=education in the circle of arts and sciences}}, from {{der|en|grc|ἐγκύκλιος||[[circular]]}} + {{m|grc|παιδείᾱ||[[childrearing]]; [[education]]}}, q.v. ===Noun===
{{en-noun|s|encyclopediae|encyclopediæ}}

# {{senseid|en|Q5292}}A [[comprehensive]] [[reference work]] (often spanning several printed volumes) with articles (usually arranged in alphabetical order, or sometimes arranged by category) on a range of [[subjects]], sometimes general, sometimes limited to a particular field.
#: {{ux|en|I only use the library for the '''encyclopedia''', as we’ve got most other books here.}}
#: {{ux|en|His life's work is a four-volume '''encyclopedia''' of aviation topics.}}
# [[similarly|Similarly]] [[comprehensive]] [[work]]s in other [[format]]s.
#: {{ux|en|{{w|Wikipedia}} is an online '''encyclopedia''' anyone can edit.&lt;sup&gt;&lt;small&gt;[citation needed]&lt;/small&gt;&lt;/sup&gt;}}
# {{lb|en|dated}} The [[circle]] of [[arts]] and [[sciences]]; a [[comprehensive]] summary of [[knowledge]], or of a branch of [[knowledge]].

====Usage notes====
* The spelling ''encyclopedia'' is standard in American English, common in Canadian English, accepted in Australian and International English, and also very common in British English although [[nonstandard]]. Oxford spelling prefers the etymologized form ''encyclopaedia'', which restores the ''αι'' diphthong in Ancient Greek ''παιδεία''. The variant with the ''æ'' ligature still appears in the titles of some encyclopaedic works, but it is otherwise archaic in ordinary usage.
